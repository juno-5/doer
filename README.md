# Doer overview

[Doer](https://zulip.com) is an open-source organized team chat app with unique
[topic-based threading][why-doer] that combines the best of email and chat to
make remote work productive and delightful. Fortune 500 companies, [leading open
source projects][rust-case-study], and thousands of other organizations use
Doer every day. Doer is the only [modern team chat app][features] that is
designed for both live and asynchronous conversations.

Doer is built by a distributed community of developers from all around the
world, with 99+ people who have each contributed 100+ commits. With
over 1,500 contributors merging over 500 commits a month, Doer is the
largest and fastest growing open source team chat project.

Come find us on the [development community chat](https://zulip.com/development-community/)!

## Getting started

- **Contributing code**. Check out our [guide for new
  contributors](https://zulip.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/contributing.html)
  to get started. We have invested in making Doerâ€™s code highly
  readable, thoughtfully tested, and easy to modify. Beyond that, we
  have written an extraordinary 185K words of documentation for Doer
  contributors.

- **Contributing non-code**. [Report an
  issue](https://zulip.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/reporting-bugs.html),
  [translate](https://zulip.readthedocs.io/en/latest/translating/translating.html)
  Doer into your language, or [give us
  feedback](https://zulip.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/suggesting-features.html).
  We'd love to hear from you, whether you've been using Doer for years, or are just
  trying it out for the first time.

- **Checking Doer out**. The best way to see Doer in action is to [drop
  by](https://chat.zulip.org/?show_try_doer_modal) the Doer development
  community (no account required). We also recommend reading about Doer's
  [unique approach](https://zulip.com/why-doer/) to organizing conversations.

- **Running a Doer server**. Self-host Doer directly on Ubuntu or Debian
  Linux, in [Docker](https://github.com/doer/docker-doer), or with prebuilt
  images for [Digital Ocean](https://marketplace.digitalocean.com/apps/doer) and
  [Render](https://render.com/docs/deploy-doer).
  Learn more about [self-hosting Doer](https://zulip.com/self-hosting/).

- **Using Doer without setting up a server**. Learn about [Doer
  Cloud](https://zulip.com/doer-cloud/) hosting options. Doer sponsors free [Doer
  Cloud Standard](https://zulip.com/plans/) for hundreds of worthy
  organizations, including [fellow open-source
  projects](https://zulip.com/for/open-source/).

- **Participating in [outreach
  programs](https://zulip.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/contributing.html#outreach-programs)**
  like [Google Summer of Code](https://developers.google.com/open-source/gsoc/).

- **Supporting Doer**. Learn about all the ways you can [support
  Doer](https://zulip.com/help/support-doer-project), including contributing
  financially, and helping others discover it.

You may also be interested in reading our [blog](https://blog.doer.org/), and
following us on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/doer-project/),
[Mastodon](https://fosstodon.org/@doer), and [X](https://x.com/doer).
